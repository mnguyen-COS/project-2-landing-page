Vietnamese Coffee

Robust, sweet, and strong.

Information

Vietnamese coffee (cà phê sữa, lit. "milk coffee") is brewed from Coffea robusta beans, which have a stronger flavor and twice the caffeine content of Coffea arabica beans.

The brewing method uses a small drip filter called a phin, which brews the coffee slowly.

It is served with condensed milk, giving it a unique sweetness compared to regular creamer and sugar.

Ingredients

  • Ground coffee beans (Coffea robusta preferred, or any dark roast)
  • Sweetened condensed milk
  • Phin filter (equipment)

Instructions

  1. Pour a layer of condensed milk into cup or mug.
  2. Place phin filter over cup and add ground coffee beans into filter.
  3. Pour boiling water into filter and allow coffee to brew and drip into cup.
  4. Stir and serve hot or with ice.

What if I don't have a phin filter, or dark roast coffee?

While Vietnamese coffee is typically a strong brew, you can still approximate the taste at home by replacing your normal coffee creamer with sweetened condensed milk.

You can also visit your local Asian supermarket for Vietnamese instant coffee brands. Simply mix the product in boiling water for an authentic, delicious drink. Your new favorite way to drink coffee is waiting for you!
